RISK-OFF 'Extreme Fear' in crypto and persistent ETF outflows continue to weigh on risk appetite.

On the final day of June, markets are displaying a sharp divergence: Wall Street rallied on easing US-Iran tensions, while the crypto market remains firmly in Extreme Fear territory. The Fear & Greed index stands at 15, near historic lows, as BTC has shed 19.8% over the past month and is currently trading at $59,143. On the macro front, the US 10-year Treasury yield has pulled back to 4.37%, and the VIX at 17.6 suggests relatively contained equity stress — yet the stock rally is failing to lift crypto. The DXY is modestly firmer near 101.4, while the Coinbase premium remains clearly negative at -0.19, signaling that institutional buyers have yet to step in. Looking ahead, tomorrow's ISM Manufacturing PMI, Wednesday's ADP employment report, and Thursday's Non-Farm Payrolls will be pivotal in shaping Fed rate expectations for the remainder of the year.
