RISK-OFF Record crypto ETF outflows, 'Extreme Fear' index, and escalating geopolitical tensions keep markets firmly in risk-off territory.

As of June 28, 2026, global markets are navigating a pronounced risk-off environment. BTC is struggling to hold $60,148 with a 7-day loss of 6.2%, while total crypto market capitalization hovers around $2.16 trillion. The Fear & Greed Index sits deep in Extreme Fear at 18 — a level that historically signals peak capitulation zones, though the technical picture has yet to confirm a reversal. On the macro front, VIX eased to 18.41 and the 10-year US Treasury yield dipped to 4.37%; the 2Y-10Y spread turning positive at 28 basis points suggests recession fears are moderating. However, NASDAQ 100 futures fell 1.2% while semiconductor ETF SMH dropped 3.97%, reflecting persistent pressure on the tech sector. Key upcoming data releases — ISM Manufacturing PMI (July 1), ADP Employment, and the critical Non-Farm Payrolls (July 2, forecast: 114K vs. prior 172K) — will be the primary catalysts for near-term direction; a significant miss could further dampen risk appetite. Meanwhile, the Hormuz tanker attack and escalating Iran-US tensions are adding geopolitical uncertainty, even as Brent crude fell 3.53% to $72.60.
